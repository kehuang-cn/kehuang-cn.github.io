Ke Huang is a PhD student at Xi’an Jiaotong-Liverpool University. His research interests lie broadly in **Embodied AI**, **Spatial Intelligence**, **3D/4D Scene Representation**, and **Robust Perception for Dynamic Environments**. He is particularly interested in developing efficient and reliable visual representations for dynamic scene understanding, reconstruction, and interaction.

Previously, he received his bachelor's degree from **Xi’an Jiaotong-Liverpool University** and his master's degree from **University College London (UCL)**.

His recent work includes a **CVPR** paper:
- [Paper on arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20807)

He is open to academic collaboration and research discussion in areas related to embodied perception, spatial intelligence, and dynamic scene understanding.
